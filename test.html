CHAPTER 3 DESIGNATED PILOT EXAMINERS

Section 1 Designate/Renew a General Aviation Pilot Examiner

13-136  PROGRAM TRACKING AND REPORTING SUBSYSTEM (PTRS) ACTIVITY CODES.

A.     Large/Turbine Aircraft: 1551.

B.     Other Aircraft: 1552.

13-137  OBJECTIVE. The objective of this task is to determine if an individual meets the qualifications for designation or renewal as a designated pilot examiner (DPE). Completion of this task results in designation, renewal, reinstatement, termination, or nonrenewal of a pilot examiner.

13-138  GENERAL.

A.     Purpose. This chapter provides procedures for the designation, renewal, reinstatement, termination, and nonrenewal of a pilot examiner designation.

B.     Definitions (as used in this chapter).

1)      Applicant. A person being tested for a certificate or rating.
2)      Candidate. An individual being tested or considered for designation as a pilot examiner.
3)      Vintage aircraft. A large, type‑certificated aircraft, no longer in significant commercial service.
4)      DPE. A designated representative of the Administrator who is authorized by Title 49 of the United States Code (49 U.S.C.), chapter 447, section 44702, to perform those tasks described in Title 14 of the Code of Federal Regulations (14 CFR) part 183, § 183.23.
5)      High activity DPE. A pilot examiner who conducts at least 50 practical tests (aircraft or simulator) in a given quarter.
6)      National Designated Flight Engineer Examiner (NDFEER). A flight engineer examiner who is authorized to conduct airman certification and proficiency practical tests in vintage airplanes in accordance with the provisions of the National Designated Pilot Examiner Registry (NDPER)/NDFEER program.
7)      Experimental aircraft examiner (EAE). A pilot examiner who is designated by the Administrator to conduct airman practical tests in aircraft for which the Operating Limitations require a Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) issued authorization to act as pilot in command (PIC). EAEs are authorized to issue these authorizations.
8)      NDPER examiner. A pilot examiner who is authorized to conduct airman certification and proficiency practical tests in vintage airplanes in accordance with the provisions of the NDPER/NDFEER program.
9)      Specialty aircraft examiner (SAE). A nationally designated examiner authorized to conduct practical tests in vintage or experimental aircraft. An SAE may be authorized to conduct type rating practical tests, authorization practical tests, or both. An SAE may be selected to serve the NDPER or NDFEER.

C.     Privileges. A DPE is authorized to perform the following:

1)      Accept applications and conduct practical tests leading to the issuance of pilot and flight instructor certificates and/or ratings in the type of aircraft appropriate to the certificates and letters of authority held by the examiner.
2)      Issue temporary pilot/flight instructor certificates to applicants the examiner has tested and found qualified for a certificate or rating.
3)      Issue FAA Form 8060‑5, Notice of Disapproval of Application, to applicants whose performance, during a practical test conducted by that examiner, did not meet the applicable practical test standards (PTS) for a certificate or rating.
4)      Issue letters of discontinuance when the practical test is terminated because of situations such as unforecast weather, the applicant or examiner becomes physically incapacitated, the aircraft has mechanical difficulties after the test has begun, or other unanticipated situations.
5)      Accept applications and issue student pilot certificates.
6)      Accept an application when the airman has an FAA Form 8500‑15, Statement of Demonstrated Ability (SODA). The examiner must verify the appropriate limitations relating to the SODA with the Flight Standards District Office (FSDO). Any appropriate limitations relating to operational privileges must be placed on the airman certificate. See Volume 5, Chapter 8, Conduct a Special Medical Test: Part 67, for examples of operational limitations.

C.     Commercial Pilot Examiner (CE). A CE conducts practical tests for commercial pilot certification and additional ratings.

D.    Airline Transport Pilot Examiner (ATPE). An ATPE conducts airline transport pilot (ATP) practical tests for the original issuance of an ATP certificate and additional ratings as specifically authorized/limited. ATPEs must hold category and class, and, if appropriate, type ratings on their pilot certificates for the tests to be conducted.

1)      ATPEs are not authorized to conduct instrument rating practical tests unless they hold a CIRE designation for that category and class of aircraft.

A.     General Criteria. In addition to meeting the professional standards in the preceding paragraph, an examiner candidate must meet the following standards:

1)      Be at least 21 years old.
2)      Have a good record as a pilot and flight instructor in regard to accidents, incidents, and violations.
3)      Have a reputation for integrity and dependability in the industry and the community.
4)      Have a history of cooperation with the FAA.
5)      Hold a valid third‑class medical certificate for initial designation, except in the case of a designation limited to examining in balloons, gliders, or flight simulators. No medical certificate is required for examining in balloons, gliders, and flight simulators.
6)      Serve as a flight instructor, or serve in another comparable position for a flight school, executive operator, air carrier operator, or a commercial operator, or serve as a check airman or check pilot for a part 121, 125, or 135 operator or for a commercial operator.

B.     Examiner Applications. Except as indicated in subparagraphs 3 and 4 below, candidates should send a completed FAA Form 8710‑10 and copies of their pilot, flight instructor, and medical certificates requesting examiner designation(s) to the NEB. The NEB accepts and evaluates applications from persons who apply for initial designation. The NEB will advise the candidate by letter whether or not the candidate meets the criteria for the designation sought. If the candidate does not meet the applicable criteria, the NEB will advise the candidate on what eligibility criteria was not met. The candidate should retain a copy of all documents submitted to the NEB for his or her personal records.

1)      The NEB evaluates an examiner candidates FAA Form 8710‑10 application for general qualification eligibility, technical qualifications, and aviation experience and skills for the examiner designation sought. An examiner must have logged at least 5 hours as PIC in each make and model of multiengine airplane and helicopters in which that examiner conducts practical tests. This eligibility criteria is the responsibility of the examiner that must be met before being allowed to conduct a practical test in a specific make and model of multiengine airplane and helicopters. The supervising FSDO must ensure that an examiner meets this eligibility criteria before authorizing that examiner to conduct a practical test in a specific make and model of multiengine airplane and helicopters. The NEB does not evaluate an examiner candidate for meeting this eligibility requirement.
2)      If extenuating circumstances justify the exception, the NEB may approve a DPE candidate who does not meet all of the qualification requirements of Order 8710.3 for assignment to the national examiner pool. However, a statement explaining the deficiency and detailing the circumstances, which justify approval of the application, is made a part of the candidates file.
3)      Candidates for designation as a PPE, ACR, MC/FPE, or TCE send their completed application forms directly to the designating FSDO for processing. (Candidates holding PPE, ACR, MC/FPE, or TCE designations and/or applying for additional designation as a DPE must submit applications for DPE authorization to the NEB.)
4)      Candidates for designation as an NDPER examiner should submit applications directly to the EAA for consideration. The EAA will recommend successful candidates to the NPO for selection and appointment.
5)      Candidates for renewal or reinstatement should send applications directly to the jurisdictional FSDO; however, a relocating examiner whose designation is not current must apply for reinstatement at the new FSDO through the NEB. The application is processed as though it were an initial application.
6)      Candidates for designation as an SAE or EAE should submit copies of their applications to the national program manager for consideration after the NEB has notified them that their application has been accepted.

3)      Proceeds with the preflight briefing and the practical test.

13-161  PRACTICAL TEST OF DPE CANDIDATE WITH ACTUAL APPLICANT (INSPECTOR OBSERVING).

A.     Pretest Activities.

1)      The ASI informs both the DPE candidate and applicant that the ASI makes the final decision regarding the certification of the airman and the designation of the pilot examiner.
3)      The ASI ensures that both the DPE candidate and the applicant have the required documents (listed in paragraph 13‑160D). The DPE candidate should check all pertinent records to ensure that the applicant meets the requirements for the certificate or the rating sought.
4)      If a discrepancy that cannot be immediately corrected exists in any of the aircraft documents, the ASI returns all submitted documents to the applicant. The ASI informs the applicant of the reasons for ineligibility and explains how the applicant may correct the discrepancies.

B.     Observation of Practical Test. If the DPE candidate and applicant meet all prerequisites for the rating or certificate sought, the ASI has the DPE candidate conduct the practical test for the certificate or rating the applicant is seeking.

·        Both the DPE candidate and the applicant perform satisfactorily.

·        The DPE candidate performs satisfactorily but the applicant is unsatisfactory.

·        The DPE candidate performs unsatisfactorily but the applicant performs satisfactorily.

·        Both the DPE candidate and the applicant perform unsatisfactorily.

A.     DPE Candidate and Applicant Satisfactory. If both the candidate and applicant perform satisfactorily, the ASI accomplishes the following:

·        Observes the DPE candidate debrief the applicant.

·        Issues a temporary airman certificate to the applicant (see the chapter related to the certificate or rating involved).

B.     DPE Candidate Satisfactory/Applicant Unsatisfactory.

1)      The ASI observes the DPE candidate debrief the applicant on the unsatisfactory results of the practical test, and the ASI then issues FAA Form 8060‑5 to the applicant.
2)      For the DPE candidate, the ASI follows the procedures outlined in subparagraph 13‑162A.

C.     DPE Candidate Unsatisfactory/Applicant Satisfactory.

1)      The ASI debriefs the applicant and issues one of the following:

·        A letter of discontinuance (Figure 13‑21), giving credit for all pilot operations completed satisfactorily; or

·        A temporary airman certificate, if appropriate.

2)      The ASI debriefs the DPE candidate on the deficiencies and explains that the standards for a pilot examiner were not met and that the designation will not be issued or renewed. If renewal of a designation is denied, refer to paragraph 13‑149 for additional procedures. The ASI completes the reverse side of FAA Form 8710‑1 and forwards the applicants certification file to AFS‑760.
3)       The ASI informs the office manager that the DPE candidates performance was unsatisfactory and that the designation should not be issued or renewed.
4)      The ASI prepares a letter of denial for the FSDO managers signature (figure 13‑22). For a renewal, all procedures must be accomplished IAW paragraph 13‑145.
5)      The ASI closes PTRS.

D.    DPE Candidate Unsatisfactory/Applicant Unsatisfactory.

1)      The ASI debriefs the applicant and issues FAA Form 8060‑5, giving the applicant credit for satisfactory items.
2)      The ASI debriefs the DPE candidate on the deficiencies and explains that the standards for a pilot examiner were not met and that the designation will not be issued or renewed. If renewal of a designation is denied, refers to paragraph 13‑149 for additional procedures. The ASI completes the reverse side of FAA Form 8710‑1 and forwards the applicants certification file to AFS‑760.
3)      The ASI informs the office manager that the DPE candidates performance was unsatisfactory and that the designation should not be issued or renewed.

13-163  PRACTICAL TEST OF A DPE CANDIDATE WITH THE INSPECTOR ACTING AS AN APPLICANT. The ASI conducting the practical test or an Airworthiness ASI should review aircraft maintenance records. The Airworthiness ASI reviews the logbooks, the Airworthiness Certificate, and the registration to determine if the aircraft is airworthy and suitable for use in the practical test. After review, the ASI returns the documents. The Operations ASI assumes the role of an applicant for a certificate or rating and instructs the DPE candidate to conduct the practical test as if the ASI were an applicant, including a preflight briefing, oral portion, a flight, and a postflight debriefing.

A.     Oral Portion. During the oral portion of the practical test, the ASI accomplishes the following:

2)      Determines whether the DPE candidate asks questions that have only one correct answer.
3)      Determines whether the DPE candidate can recognize incorrect answers and take appropriate action; for example, termination of the practical test and issuance of a notice of disapproval of application.

B.     Flight and/or Simulator Portion. During the flight and/or simulator portion of the practical test, the ASI accomplishes the following:

1)      Determines whether the DPE candidate requests maneuvers from the appropriate PTS and applies the criteria from the PTS.
2)      Determines if the DPE candidate can recognize and correct the applicants errors and take appropriate action; for example, termination of the practical test and issuance of a notice of disapproval of application.

C.     Debriefing. During the postflight debriefing, the ASI asks the DPE candidate if the applicant passed or failed and on what the DPE candidate has based this decision. If the ASI has deliberately not performed to the standards and the DPE candidate did not recognize this, the ASI informs the DPE candidate that an examiner designation cannot be issued.

1)      If the DPE candidate is successful, the ASI has the DPE candidate fill out a dummy temporary airman certificate or notice of disapproval of application to ensure that the DPE candidate is aware of the proper procedures. Failure to fill out the forms is not disqualifying; the ASI should instruct the DPE candidate on the correct procedures. The ASI ensures that any dummy certificate is then destroyed.
2)      The ASI issues the DPE candidates pilot examiner designation, as follows:
a)      Completes the reverse side of FAA Form 8710‑1 and files it in the examiners FSDO file (do not forward file to AFS‑760);
b)      Issues FAA Form 8430‑9 (figure 13‑18) and types the appropriate designation on the certificate, signed by the FSDO manager;
c)      Issues the LOA, if appropriate, signed by the FSDO manager (figure 13‑15)
d)      Issues FAA Form 8000‑5 (figure 13‑17), signed by the FSDO manager; and
e)      Issues a supply of the following items to the DPE candidate:

·        FAA Form 8060‑4;

·        FAA Form 8060‑5;

·        FAA Form 8710‑1;

·        FAA Form 8710‑2, Student Pilot Certificate; and

·        Any aviation safety handouts that would be of assistance.

f)        Notify AFS‑760 if designee has FIRE authority.
4)      The ASI closes the PTRS.

13-164  POSTFLIGHT PROCEDURES.

C.     Notify NEB. The ASI notifies the NEB in writing that the candidate was satisfactory or unsatisfactory, as appropriate.

D.    PTRS. The ASI completes PTRS IAW the PTRS Procedures Manual.

13-165  ADDITIONAL DESIGNATIONS. An additional designation may be issued to an examiner by the same practical test procedures used for original designation.

A.     Schedule Appointment. The ASI should refer to subparagraph 13‑160D.

B.     Evaluate the DPE Candidate. The ASI may evaluate the DPE candidates suitability for additional designation by using one of the methods listed below. The methods are listed in order of preference.

1)      The ASI observes a complete practical test. For the initial issuance of an additional designation, the practical test must be accomplished in an aircraft that can carry an FAA ASI. The ASI will observe a complete practical test in which both oral and flight/simulator portions of the test are accomplished.
2)      The ASI may act in the role of an applicant for a practical test.
3)      The ASI may test the DPE examiner candidate on selected maneuvers to assess the examiners flight proficiency and ability to evaluate to the applicable test standards as a pilot examiner.

13-166  RENEWALINSPECTOR TECHNICAL ADMINISTRATION.

A.     Application for Renewal. Examiners should apply for renewal at least 60 days before their certificate expires. If the examiner has not reapplied 45 days before the expiration date, the office should send the examiner a reminder letter. The examiner must provide a letter and other documents listed in subparagraph 13‑139F9) to the FSDO.

B.     ASIs Action. When renewing a designation, the ASI should prepare and issue a new Form 8430‑9, listing the designations renewed. This form expires at the end of the 12th calendar‑month after the month of issue. A new display type certificate is not needed.

13-167  CANCELLATION. In cases when an examiner is suspected of activity for which emergency cancellation of the designation is necessary to ensure safety, FSDOs may direct the examiner, in writing, to immediately cease all testing and certification activity pending FAA investigation. Upon determination of the circumstances of such incidents, FSDOs should initiate termination or nonrenewal action, if indicated, in strict accordance with the procedures and actions stated in paragraph 13‑149.

13-168  NONRENEWAL OR TERMINATION. It is preferable to terminate an examiners services by not renewing the designation, rather than by cancellation of the designation during its 1‑year duration. Such decisions should be coordinated with regional counsel. Additional guidance for terminating general aviation pilot examiner designations is in paragraphs 13‑149 and 150.

A.     Coordination. FSDO personnel should coordinate all contemplated nonrenewals or terminations with the regional Flight Standards division manager and the regional assistant chief counsel before initiating such action. Regional Flight Standards divisions should monitor FSDO implementation of such actions.

B.     Notification. Examiners should be notified in writing of the reasons for the proposed action. The reasons for nonrenewal or termination should be as specific as possible, cite applicable regulations and handbook guidance, and must be supported by examples, especially in cases of unacceptable conduct.

C.     Right to Redress. The written notification should give the examiner the option to respond in writing or in person. The notification should advise the examiner that if responding in person, legal counsel may accompany him/her. The notification should also inform the examiner that a record must be made of any meeting held.
